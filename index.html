I am a senior undergraduate student in Computer Engineering at Ho Chi Minh City University of Technology (HCMUT), currently ranked #1 in my department with a GPA of 9.27/10.0. I am applying for PhD programs in Medical AI and Computer Vision for Fall 2026.

Currently, I work as a Research Assistant at Xu Lab (Carnegie Mellon University), advised by Prof. Min Xu, and at the Machine and Hybrid Intelligence Lab (Northwestern University), advised by Prof. Ulas Bagci. My recent work explores Mixture-of-Experts architectures for semi-supervised histopathology segmentation and stain-structure disentanglement for robust pathology analysis.

Research Vision

My research aims to bridge the gap between advanced computer vision and clinical reality. Specifically, I focus on three interconnected pillars:

Foundation-Model-Driven Design

Leveraging large-scale pre-trained models (e.g., SAM) through principled adaptation strategies rather than naive fine-tuning.

Mathematically Grounded Modeling

Building theoretically motivated architectures‚Äîincluding Mixture-of-Experts, disentangled representations, and diffusion models.

Learning with Imperfect Annotations

Developing robust AI systems that operate effectively with limited, noisy, or domain-shifted medical data.

My goal is to build robust, generalizable AI systems for medical imaging that can translate from controlled research environments to the heterogeneous conditions of real-world clinical practice.

selected publications

  1. BMVC
    Semi-MoE: Mixture-of-Experts meets Semi-Supervised Histopathology Segmentation
    Vi Vu*, Tuan-Hung Nguyen*, and others
    In British Machine Vision Conference (BMVC), 2025
    Abs Bib PDF Code

    We propose Semi-MoE, a novel framework that leverages Mixture-of-Experts to address the homogeneity problem in semi-supervised histopathology segmentation. Our method introduces specialized expert networks that handle diverse domain distributions in pathology images, improving segmentation performance under limited annotations.

  2. CVPRW
    HDC: Hierarchical Distillation for Multi-level Noisy Consistency in Semi-supervised Histopathology Segmentation
    Vi Vu*, Tran-Quang-Khai Le*, and others
    In IEEE/CVF Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition Workshops (CVPRW), 2025
    Abs Bib PDF Code

    We introduce HDC, a hierarchical distillation framework that addresses multi-level noise in pseudo-labels for semi-supervised histopathology segmentation. Our approach employs a cascaded consistency regularization strategy that progressively refines predictions across multiple semantic levels.

  3. MICCAI-W
    Learning Disentangled Stain and Structural Representations for Robust Histopathology Analysis
    Vi Vu*, Huy-Hoang Pham*, and others
    In MICCAI Workshop on Computational Pathology (COMPAYL), 2025
    Abs Bib PDF Code

    We propose a mean-teacher framework for disentangling stain appearance from structural content in histopathology images. By separating H&E staining variations from underlying tissue morphology, our method achieves robust segmentation performance across diverse staining protocols with minimal labeled data.
