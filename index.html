I am an aspiring Ph.D. researcher in Medical AI and Computer Vision, currently serving as a Research Assistant at Carnegie Mellon University (Xu Lab) and Northwestern University (MHI Lab), where I work at the intersection of mathematically grounded modeling and clinically meaningful applications.

I hold a B.E. in Computer Engineering from Ho Chi Minh City University of Technology, where I graduated as the valedictorian in the department with a GPA of 9.27/10.0. I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Min Xu, Dr. Xingjian Li, and Prof. Ulas Bagci, whose mentorship has shaped my interest in rigorous, impact-driven research.

Research Focus

My research philosophy centers on mathematically grounded modeling and foundation-model-driven designs. I am particularly interested in tackling learning with imperfect annotations (semi/unsupervised learning) to bridge the gap between advanced vision models and clinical reality.

selected publications

  1. BMVC
    Semi-MoE: Mixture-of-Experts meets Semi-Supervised Histopathology Segmentation
    Vi Vu*, TH Nguyen*, and others
    In British Machine Vision Conference (BMVC), 2025
    Abs Bib PDF Code

    We propose Semi-MoE, a novel framework that leverages Mixture-of-Experts to address the homogeneity problem in semi-supervised histopathology segmentation. Our method introduces specialized expert networks that handle diverse domain distributions in pathology images, improving segmentation performance under limited annotations.

  2. CVPRW
    HDC: Hierarchical Distillation for Multi-level Noisy Consistency in Semi-Supervised Fetal Ultrasound Segmentation
    Vi Vu*, TQK Le*, and others
    In IEEE/CVF Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition Workshops (CVPRW), 2025
    Abs Bib PDF Code

    We introduce HDC, a hierarchical distillation framework that addresses multi-level noise in pseudo-labels for semi-supervised histopathology segmentation. Our approach employs a cascaded consistency regularization strategy that progressively refines predictions across multiple semantic levels.

  3. MICCAI-W
    Learning Disentangled Stain and Structural Representations for Semi-Supervised Histopathology Segmentation
    Vi Vu*, HH Pham*, and others
    In MICCAI Workshop on Computational Pathology (COMPAYL), 2025
    Abs Bib PDF Code

    We propose a mean-teacher framework for disentangling stain appearance from structural content in histopathology images. By separating H&E staining variations from underlying tissue morphology, our method achieves robust segmentation performance across diverse staining protocols with minimal labeled data.

  4. ISBI
    Fetal-BCP: Addressing Empirical Distribution Gap in Semi-Supervised Fetal Ultrasound Segmentation
    HH Pham, HT Nguyen, Vi Vu, and 1 more author
    In IEEE International Symposium on Biomedical Imaging (ISBI), 2025
    Challenge Winner Abs Bib PDF Code

    Winner of Fetal Ultrasound Grand Challenge at ISBI 2025

    We propose Fetal-BCP, a boundary-consistent pseudo-labeling framework for fetal ultrasound segmentation that addresses the empirical distribution gap between labeled and unlabeled data. Our method leverages boundary-aware consistency regularization to improve segmentation accuracy in challenging prenatal imaging scenarios.
